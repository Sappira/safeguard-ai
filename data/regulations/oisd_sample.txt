OISD Standard 105 - Work Permit System

A work permit system is mandatory for all non-routine maintenance and operational activities in hazardous areas. No hot work shall be carried out in areas where the gas concentration exceeds 10% of the Lower Explosive Limit without explicit written authorization from the site safety officer.

Confined space entry requires atmospheric testing prior to entry. Oxygen levels must be between 19.5% and 23.5% for safe entry. Gas concentrations must be below 10% LEL. Continuous monitoring is mandatory throughout the confined space operation.

Hot work permits must not be issued within 15 metres of any active gas release point or any area where gas readings exceed 25% LEL. Any sudden increase in gas readings during an active hot work permit must result in immediate suspension of all ignition sources and evacuation of the area.

OISD Standard 116 - Fire Protection Facilities

All industrial zones must maintain functioning gas detection systems calibrated quarterly. Alarm thresholds must be set at 20% LEL for first alarm and 40% LEL for evacuation alarm. Any detector reading above 60% LEL must trigger automatic process shutdown protocols.

Shift changeovers in hazardous zones must be documented with incoming and outgoing supervisors both signing the handover log. No shift handover is considered complete without a physical walkthrough of active work permits.

Visakhapatnam Steel Plant Incident Reference - January 2025

Post-incident analysis identified three concurrent conditions that individually did not trigger automated shutdown: gas pressure sensor readings at 58% LEL, an active maintenance permit in the coke oven battery area, and a shift changeover in progress with reduced supervision coverage. The investigation concluded that a compound risk evaluation system, had one existed, would have flagged this combination as critical risk approximately 47 minutes before the explosion occurred.

Factory Act 1948 - Section 36 and 37

Explosive or inflammable dust, gas or vapour must be controlled through adequate ventilation and where practicable through elimination at source. Where gas accumulation cannot be eliminated, continuous atmospheric monitoring with automatic alerting is required. Workers must be trained on evacuation procedures specific to the hazardous materials present in their assigned zone.

No worker shall be permitted to enter a confined space where the atmosphere has not been tested and certified safe within the preceding one hour. Re-testing is mandatory after any process change or after a period exceeding two hours since the last test.
